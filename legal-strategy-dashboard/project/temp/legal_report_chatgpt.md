# Phase 2 — Strategic legal & risk analysis (with statute/primary-source anchoring)

## 2.1 Offence architecture and viable defence levers

### A) Drug driving (presence) — Victorian framework

Road Safety Act offence architecture includes “drug present” and “refusal” pathways. The Act’s drug-testing provisions and refusal consequences are embedded in s49 and related procedural provisions. ([content.legislation.vic.gov.au](https://content.legislation.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2025-12/86-127aa234-authorised.pdf "https://content.legislation.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2025-12/86-127aa234-authorised.pdf"))

**Key point:** If you are facing s49 drug-driving counts (including “drug present”), those are treated as serious risk factors in Commonwealth vetting (criminal conduct + drug use). ([Protective Security Policy Framework](https://www.protectivesecurity.gov.au/system/files/2025-07/australian-government-personnel-security-adjudicative-standard-2025.pdf "https://www.protectivesecurity.gov.au/system/files/2025-07/australian-government-personnel-security-adjudicative-standard-2025.pdf"))

**Defence engineering implication:**  
Your strategy should be structured into two “compartments”:

1.  **Status/administrative error** compartment (licence suspension / database mismatch → s30-type risk).
    
2.  **Drug/refusal** compartment (s49 presence + refusal → highest legal and vetting risk).
    

Conflating them weakens both. Separate, then link them only through credibility + pattern.

### B) Refusal counts: the narrow statutory opening

Refusal provisions operate through s49 and related test-compliance mechanisms. The Road Safety Act contains a statutory concept that a person must not be convicted of refusal if they had a “reason of a substantial character” (excluding mere desire to avoid detection). ([content.legislation.vic.gov.au](https://content.legislation.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2025-12/86-127aa234-authorised.pdf "https://content.legislation.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2025-12/86-127aa234-authorised.pdf"))

**Strategic use for your “harassment → refusal” narrative**

-   The legal hurdle is that the court will scrutinise whether the refusal was **genuinely safety/medical/compulsion-based** versus avoidance.
    
-   Therefore: your refusal defence must be framed as a _specific, immediate, articulable reason_ (e.g., acute distress/panic response, fear of continued unlawful detention, medical episode, inability to comply) and supported by contemporaneous conduct:
    
    -   complaint lodged same day (9-Feb)

## 2.2 Diversion reality-check (this is pivotal)

Your documents reference diversion via the Criminal Procedure Act. ([Protective Security Policy Framework](https://www.protectivesecurity.gov.au/system/files/2025-07/australian-government-personnel-security-adjudicative-standard-2025.pdf "https://www.protectivesecurity.gov.au/system/files/2025-07/australian-government-personnel-security-adjudicative-standard-2025.pdf"))  
However, the Criminal Procedure Act’s diversion-adjournment provision is expressly constrained: it states the diversion-adjournment power does **not apply** to certain Road Safety Act s49 offences. ([content.legislation.vic.gov.au](https://content.legislation.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2026-02/09-7aa108-authorised.pdf "https://content.legislation.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2026-02/09-7aa108-authorised.pdf"))

**Consequence:**

-   If your active counts include **s49** drug-driving/refusal, **diversion is not a reliable primary plan**.
    
-   You must treat diversion as **Plan C** (only if prosecution and court structure permits a re-framing to non-s49 outcomes), and you must build **Plan A / Plan B** that do not rely on diversion.
    

**Practical pivot:**  
Your negotiation target becomes:

-   **Withdraw / amend s49 counts** (especially refusal counts) in exchange for:
    
    -   agreed undertaking conditions,
        
    -   compliance proof,
        
    -   limited plea to a non-s49 regulatory/traffic matter where possible,
        
    -   or adjournment to obtain confirmatory evidence (brief + audit logs + lab/chain).
